## The Anatomy of a Profitable Ad Simulation

A professional and interactive ROAS calculator (such as the one available at [adsroicalc.com](https://adsroicalc.com)) requires you to enter a series of input data (*inputs*) and automatically calculates the resulting business metrics (*outputs*).

### Essential Input Data (Inputs):
1. **Estimated Budget (Ad Spend):** The capital you plan to invest in the campaign.
2. **Projected Average CPC (Cost per Click):** What you expect to pay for each visit to your website.
3. **Web Conversion Rate (CR %):** The estimated percentage of visits that will purchase the product.
4. **Average Order Value (AOV):** The average selling price of the product (VAT included or excluded, depending on what you prefer to simulate).
5. **Product and Operational Cost (Margins):** Percentage of COGS and unit shipping costs.

### Key Output Metrics (Outputs):
* **Projected Traffic:** Total clicks your website will receive.
* **Estimated Sales:** Total number of completed transactions.
* **Platform ROAS:** Reported gross efficiency of the advertising channel.
* **Real Net Profit:** The net cash generated for the business after deducting all expenses and taxes.
* **Overall ROI:** The profitability percentage of the entire investment.

## The Power of "What-If" Analysis (Scenario Simulation)

One of the greatest advantages of an interactive tool is the ability to instantly model hypothetical scenarios to see how profits fluctuate when modifying a single funnel metric.

Let's analyze the simulation of three scenarios for a product with an **AOV of €60**, a **COGS + logistics of €20**, and an **investment of €3,000**:

### Scenario A: Pessimistic (Low conversion, expensive CPC)
* **CPC:** €1.20
* **Conversion Rate:** 1.0%
* **Generated Traffic:** 2,500 visits ($3,000 / 1.20$)
* **Sales:** 25 orders ($2,500 \times 1\%$)
* **Gross Revenue:** €1,500 ($25 \times 60\ \text{€}$)
* **ROAS:** 0.50x
* **Result:** **Net loss of -€2,000** (€1,500 - €3,000 Ads - €500 COGS/shipping).

### Scenario B: Moderate (Standard conversion, normal CPC)
* **CPC:** €0.70
* **Conversion Rate:** 2.0%
* **Generated Traffic:** 4,285 visits
* **Sales:** 85 orders (rounded)
* **Gross Revenue:** €5,100
* **ROAS:** 1.70x
* **Result:** **Net profit of €400** (€5,100 - €3,000 Ads - €1,700 COGS/shipping).

### Scenario C: Optimistic (Optimized CRO, cheap CPC)
* **CPC:** €0.50
* **Conversion Rate:** 3.5%
* **Generated Traffic:** 6,000 visits
* **Sales:** 210 orders
* **Gross Revenue:** €12,600
* **ROAS:** 4.20x
* **Result:** **Net profit of €5,400** (€12,600 - €3,000 Ads - €4,200 COGS/shipping).

## Key Lesson: The Exponential Impact of CRO on Profitability

If you look at the difference between Scenario B and Scenario C, you will notice that the advertising investment remained exactly the same (€3,000). However, by improving the website's conversion rate from 2.0% to 3.5% (a common increase when optimizing a landing page), **the net profit multiplied by more than 13** (going from €400 to €5,400).

An interactive ROAS calculator shows you this reality with absolute mathematical clarity, helping you prioritize the optimization of your online store before deciding to double your ad budget.
